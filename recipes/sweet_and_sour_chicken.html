Sweet and Sour Chicken

This recipe is a classic take on the beloved sweet and sour chicken, featuring crispy chicken pieces coated in a tangy sauce with vibrant bell peppers and pineapple.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks (fresh or canned)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (white or apple cider)
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (for thickening)
  • Sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)
  • Chopped green onions (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, coat the chicken pieces with cornstarch, shaking off any excess.
  2. Dip the coated chicken pieces into the beaten eggs, ensuring they are well coated.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Fry the chicken pieces in batches until golden brown and cooked through. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. In the same skillet, add sliced bell peppers and pineapple chunks. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix sugar, vinegar, ketchup, soy sauce, salt, and pepper to create the sweet and sour sauce.
  6. Pour the sauce over the bell peppers and pineapple in the skillet. Bring to a simmer.
  7. Add the fried chicken pieces to the skillet and stir to coat them evenly with the sauce.
  8. Mix the cornstarch with water to create a slurry and add it to the skillet. Stir until the sauce thickens.
  9. Serve hot, garnished with sesame seeds and chopped green onions if desired.
  10. Enjoy your homemade sweet and sour chicken with steamed rice or noodles!
